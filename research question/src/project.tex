In order to answer the previous research question, we distinguish six steps in this master’s 
thesis. These steps are shown in Figure \ref{fig:roadmap}.

\subsection*{P\&ID and Preliminary Thermodynamic Cycle Design}

In this first part, we will numerically model the thermodynamic cycle. Each student will work 
simultaneously on different blocks, determined in advance. The analysis of the cycle will include 
energy and exergy analyses to ensure optimal performance. The numerical model will be written in 
Python, and the module CoolProp will be used to access thermodynamic property tables.

\subsection*{Optimization of the Thermodynamic Design}

Assembling all the different blocks will be the purpose of the second part. Besides connecting 
the blocks, an optimization process will be developed to determine the optimal parameters under 
nominal conditions. In addition, more physical aspects will be added to the model, such as head 
loss calculations and heat transfer modeling.

\subsection*{Sizing of the Main Components}

Once the thermodynamic design is fixed, the main components, such as heat exchangers, expansion 
valves, and water tanks, will be selected.

\subsection*{Establishment of a First Control Strategy}

One main advantage of this machine will be its capacity to simulate different conditions thanks 
to the dual evaporator. A control logic must therefore be implemented. This part is critical 
because we will need to define the nature and location of the sensors.

\subsection*{CAD and Operational Model}

In this part, the other student will be in charge of the mechanical design of the machine. 
This will include material and component selection, heat exchanger and insulation design, and 
the elaboration of precise technical drawings. It will also be our task to correct the final 
cycle design (head losses, heat transfer, \dots).

\subsection*{Prototype Construction and Instrumentation}

This part will consist of constructing the prototype and implementing the necessary instrumentation.

\subsection*{Safety Validation of the Prototype}

Before starting the tests, the safety of the machine will be verified by the CREDEM platform.

\subsection*{Control Logic and Performance Validation}

Finally, the last part will be dedicated to the evaluation of the machine and its control logic. 
Several tests will take place to ensure proper operation of the machine and to collect data for 
comparison with theoretical values.
